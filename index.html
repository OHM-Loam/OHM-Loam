About

The mechanics of photography are easily learned. Seeing is not.

What matters cannot be taught: judgment, timing, restraint, and the accumulation of lived experience. These elements shape how a subject is approached and how little is required to describe it.

I am not interested in perfection as spectacle. I am interested in clarity—images that hold presence and invite a quiet connection over time.

The work is a process of reduction. What remains is intentional.
